For my first job, at a company called Xtract Solutions, I bug tested the code for allergin-mixing software for allergists who practice immunotherapy. Testing the code meant running through the same process that nurses who mixed the solutions would go through; without mixing anything myself - I only checked to make sure the math worked.

Coding, to me, could offer the assurance of a stable well-paying job with benefits, as well the potential for independently created minimal-capital startups opportunities that could transform into a working-life purpose.
